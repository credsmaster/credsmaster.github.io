# The Book of Acts
---

## Chapter 11
---

### 11.1 · Peter Explains His Actions (11:1–18)

**Key verse:** _"So then, even to Gentiles God has granted repentance that leads to life." — Acts 11:18_

#### Facts
- The apostles and believers throughout Judea hear that Gentiles have received the word of God.
- When Peter returns to Jerusalem, the circumcised believers criticize him for eating with uncircumcised men.
- Peter explains everything exactly as it happened, recounting his vision of the sheet, the arrival of Cornelius’s men, and the Holy Spirit falling on them just as He had on the Jewish believers at Pentecost.
- Hearing this, the critics quiet down and praise God for extending salvation to the Gentiles.

#### Insights
- Religious traditions and cultural conditioning run deep; even after seeing miracles, the Jerusalem church’s first reaction to the Gentile breakthrough is criticism rather than celebration.
- Peter defends his actions not with theological arguments, but with a factual testimony of what God did. Experience aligned with divine action silences legalistic opposition.

#### Deeds
- Reflect on whether your first reaction to an unconventional move of God is skepticism or curiosity. Practice listening to testimonies before passing judgment.

---

### 11.2 · The Church in Antioch (11:19–30)

**Key verse:** _"The disciples were called Christians first at Antioch." — Acts 11:26_

#### Facts
- Believers scattered by the persecution that began with Stephen travel as far as Phoenicia, Cyprus, and Antioch, initially sharing the word only with Jews.
- Some believers from Cyprus and Cyrene go to Antioch and begin speaking to Greeks also, telling them the good news about the Lord Jesus.
- The Jerusalem church sends Barnabas to Antioch; seeing evidence of God's grace, he encourages them and brings Saul from Tarsus to help teach.
- A prophet named Agabus predicts a severe famine, prompting the Antioch church to send financial relief to the elders in Judea.

#### Insights
- What looked like a tragedy (the persecution after Stephen's death) becomes the very catalyst for the international expansion of the church. God repurposes pain for proclamation.
- The name "Christian" (meaning "little Christs" or "of Christ's party") was likely given by outsiders who noticed that the only common denominator in this multi-ethnic church was Jesus Himself.

#### Deeds
- Look at a current hardship or disruption in your life. Ask God how He might be using it to scatter seeds of faith or push you into new territory.
