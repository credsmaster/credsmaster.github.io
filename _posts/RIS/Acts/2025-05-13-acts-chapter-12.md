# The Book of Acts
---

## Chapter 12
---

### 12.1 · Peter's Miraculous Escape from Prison (12:1–19)

**Key verse:** _"So Peter was kept in prison, but the church was earnestly praying to God for him." — Acts 12:5_

#### Facts
- King Herod Agrippa begins persecuting the church, executing James (the brother of John) with the sword and arresting Peter during the Festival of Unleavened Bread.
- Peter is kept in prison under heavy guard, but the church meets continuously to pray for his release.
- The night before his trial, an angel rouses a sleeping Peter, his chains fall off, and the prison gates open automatically, leading him safely into the street.
- Peter goes to the house of Mary, the mother of John Mark; the servant girl Rhoda is so overjoyed to hear his voice that she forgets to open the door, and the praying believers initially think she is crazy.

#### Insights
- Earnest prayer is powerful, yet even the early church struggled with unbelief. They were praying for Peter's release, but when he actually showed up at the door, they refused to believe it.
- God's deliverance sometimes leaves us in a state of shock. Peter himself thought he was seeing a vision until the cool night air made him realize it was reality.

#### Deeds
- When you pray for the "impossible," do you actually expect God to answer? Evaluate whether your prayers are backed by expectation or just habit.

---

### 12.2 · The Death of Herod (12:20–25)

**Key verse:** _"Immediately, because Herod did not give praise to God, an angel of the Lord struck him down, and he was eaten by worms and died." — Acts 12:23_

#### Facts
- Herod travels to Caesarea and delivers a public address to the people of Tyre and Sidon, who depend on his country for food.
- The crowd shouts, "This is the voice of a god, not of a man!"
- Because Herod accepts the praise and fails to give glory to God, an angel strikes him down, causing a gruesome death.
- Despite this political turmoil, the word of God continues to spread and flourish, and Barnabas and Saul return from Jerusalem after completing their relief mission.

#### Insights
- Pride and self-deification are catastrophic in God's eyes. Herod attempted to steal God's glory, and his swift downfall serves as a warning about the fragility of human power.
- Tyrants come and go, but the Word of God endures. The contrast between Herod's rotting body and the flourishing Word emphasizes the permanence of God's Kingdom.

#### Deeds
- Be intentional about deflecting praise toward God when you experience success or recognition. Cultivate a heart of humility.
