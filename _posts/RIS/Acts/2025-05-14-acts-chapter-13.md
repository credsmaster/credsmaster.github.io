# The Book of Acts
---

## Chapter 13
---

### 13.1 · Barnabas and Saul Sent Out (13:1–12)

**Key verse:** _"While they were worshiping the Lord and fasting, the Holy Spirit said, 'Set apart for me Barnabas and Saul for the work to which I have called them.'" — Acts 13:2_

#### Facts
- The church at Antioch has a diverse leadership team of prophets and teachers from various cultural backgrounds.
- During a time of fasting and worship, the Holy Spirit commands that Barnabas and Saul be set apart for a specific mission.
- They sail to Cyprus and preach in the synagogues; at Paphos, they encounter Elymas the sorcerer, a Jewish false prophet who tries to turn the proconsul, Sergius Paulus, away from the faith.
- Saul (now called Paul) rebukes Elymas, blinding him temporarily, which leads the proconsul to believe.

#### Insights
- Christian mission is birthed out of a corporate environment of worship and fasting, not just strategic planning meetings. The Spirit speaks when the church listens.
- Spiritual advancement often triggers immediate spiritual opposition. Elymas tried to block the gospel, but God used the confrontation to display His superior power and win over the ruler.

#### Deeds
- Dedicate a day or a specific time frame this week to fasting and worship, simply asking God to direct your steps and clarify your calling.

---

### 13.2 · Paul Preaches at Pisidian Antioch (13:13–52)

**Key verse:** _"We tell you the good news: What God promised our ancestors he has fulfilled for us, their children, by raising up Jesus." — Acts 13:32–33_

#### Facts
- Paul and his companions sail to Perga, where John Mark leaves them to return to Jerusalem.
- They go to Pisidian Antioch, and on the Sabbath, Paul stands up in the synagogue to deliver a comprehensive sermon tracing Israel's history up to Jesus.
- Paul preaches Jesus’s death, resurrection, and the forgiveness of sins, warning them not to reject God's message.
- The next Sabbath, almost the whole city gathers; Jewish leaders become jealous and abusive, prompting Paul and Barnabas to turn decisively to the Gentiles.

#### Insights
- Paul's preaching anchors Jesus firmly within Jewish history and prophetic fulfillment. Jesus is not a disruption of Israel’s story, but its ultimate climax.
- Rejection of the gospel by some often opens the door wide for others. The jealousy of the religious establishment inadvertently accelerated the inclusion of the Gentiles.

#### Deeds
- Practice summarizing the story of your faith by anchoring it in God's faithfulness over time. Be ready to share your story even when facing rejection or indifference.
