# The Book of Acts
---

## Chapter 14
---

### 14.1 · Ministry and Opposition in Iconium and Lystra (14:1–20)

**Key verse:** _"We must go through many hardships to enter the kingdom of God." — Acts 14:22_

#### Facts
- In Iconium, Paul and Barnabas preach effectively, but the city is divided, and they flee when a plot to stone them is uncovered.
- In Lystra, Paul heals a man who was lame from birth.
- Seeing this miracle, the crowd mistakes them for Greek gods, calling Barnabas Zeus and Paul Hermes, and the local priest attempts to offer sacrifices to them.
- Paul and Barnabas tear their clothes, proclaiming they are only human, and redirect the crowd's attention to the living God. Shortly after, Jews from Antioch and Iconium arrive, win over the crowd, stone Paul, and leave him for dead.

#### Insights
- The fickle nature of human praise is on full display here: the same crowd that tried to worship Paul as a god stones him and leaves him for dead shortly thereafter. Never build your identity on the applause of people.
- Paul's message to these pagans shifts from scriptural history (used with Jewish audiences) to natural theology—pointing to rains, crops, and creation as witnesses of God’s goodness.

#### Deeds
- Evaluate how you react to praise. Are you quick to redirect the credit to God, or do you internalize it? Guard your heart against seeking human approval.

---

### 14.2 · Strengthening the Churches and Returning to Antioch (14:21–28)

**Key verse:** _"And when they arrived and gathered the church together, they reported all that God had done with them and how he had opened a door of faith to the Gentiles." — Acts 14:27_

#### Facts
- Paul revives after the stoning and returns into the city, then heads to Derbe the next day with Barnabas.
- They retrace their steps through Lystra, Iconium, and Antioch, encouraging the disciples to remain true to the faith despite hardships.
- They appoint elders in each church with prayer and fasting, committing them to the Lord.
- Finally, they sail back to Antioch in Syria, where they gather the church to report on their mission and how God opened the door of faith to the Gentiles.

#### Insights
- True discipleship involves follow-up. Paul does not just convert people and abandon them; he risks his life to revisit volatile cities to strengthen and structure new communities.
- Leadership appointment in the early church was characterized by spiritual gravity (prayer and fasting) rather than mere popularity or political maneuvering.

#### Deeds
- Think about someone you recently shared your faith with or a younger believer in your life. Make an intentional effort to follow up, encourage, and strengthen them this week.
