# The Book of Acts
---

## Chapter 16
---

### 16.1 · Timothy Joins Paul & The Macedonian Call (16:1–15)

**Key verse:** _"Come over to Macedonia and help us." — Acts 16:9_

#### Facts
- Paul travels to Derbe and Lystra, where he recruits Timothy, a disciple whose mother was Jewish and father was Greek. Paul circumcises him to avoid offending the local Jews.
- They travel through Phrygia and Galatia, having been kept by the Holy Spirit from preaching the word in the province of Asia.
- During the night, Paul has a vision of a man of Macedonia begging him to come over and help them. They immediately leave for Philippi.
- On the Sabbath, they meet a group of women outside the city gate at the river, including Lydia, a dealer in purple cloth. The Lord opens her heart to respond to Paul's message, and her household is baptized.

#### Insights
- God's guidance often involves closed doors before an open one. The Holy Spirit actively blocked Paul from certain regions to ensure he was in the right place to receive the Macedonian vision.
- God works on both sides of human interactions: He sent Paul across the sea, and simultaneously opened Lydia's heart to listen. Salvation is a divine-human intersection.

#### Deeds
- If you are experiencing closed doors or frustrations in your current plans, pause and ask God if He is redirecting you toward a specific "Macedonia" you haven't considered.

### 16.2 · The Philippian Jailer Conversion (16:16–40)

**Key verse:** _"Believe in the Lord Jesus, and you will be saved—you and your household." — Acts 16:31_

#### Facts
- Paul casts a demon out of a slave girl who predicted the future, ruining her owners' business.
- The owners drag Paul and Silas before the magistrates; they are stripped, beaten with rods, and thrown into the inner cell of a prison with their feet in stocks.
- About midnight, while Paul and Silas are praying and singing hymns, a violent earthquake shakes the prison foundations, opening all doors and loosening everyone's chains.
- The jailer wakes up, draws his sword to kill himself assuming the prisoners escaped, but Paul stops him. The jailer asks how to be saved, hears the word, washes their wounds, and is baptized with his family.

#### Insights
- Worship is a weapon in adversity. Singing praises in a dark, painful dungeon turned a prison into a sanctuary and shook open physical and spiritual chains.
- The gospel completely upends social dynamics. By the end of the night, the jailer is washing the wounds of the men he had locked away, and they are eating together at his table.

#### Deeds
- When hit with sudden adversity or unfair treatment, choose to respond with prayer and praise instead of bitterness. Watch how God uses your attitude to impact those around you.
