# The Book of Acts
---

## Chapter 18
---

### 18.1 · Ministry in Corinth (18:1–17)

**Key verse:** _"Do not be afraid; keep on speaking, do not be silent. For I am with you, and no one is going to attack and harm you, because I have many people in this city." — Acts 18:9–10_

#### Facts
- Paul leaves Athens and goes to Corinth, where he meets Aquila and Priscilla, fellow tentmakers who had recently left Rome because of Claudius's edict.
- He reasons in the synagogue every Sabbath; when Silas and Timothy arrive from Macedonia, Paul devotes himself exclusively to preaching, but faces opposition and moves his base next door to the house of Titius Justus.
- Crispus, the synagogue leader, and his entire household believe and are baptized.
- God speaks to Paul in a vision, encouraging him to stay; Paul remains for a year and a half. The proconsul Gallio later dismisses a legal case brought against Paul by Jewish opponents.

#### Insights
- God provides co-workers and vocational flexibility to sustain ministry. Tentmaking was not a distraction from Paul's mission, but an integrated lifestyle strategy that linked him to Aquila and Priscilla.
- Even seasoned apostles battle fear and exhaustion. God’s direct reassurance to Paul ("Do not be afraid... I am with you") shows that personal discouragement is real, but God's presence is structural defense enough.

#### Deeds
- Recognize the value of Christian friendships and shared work. If you are feeling isolated or fearful in your environment, lean into your community and hold fast to God's promise of presence.

### 18.2 · Apollos at Ephesus and Corinth (18:18–28)

**Key verse:** _"When Priscilla and Aquila heard him, they invited him to their home and explained to him the way of God more adequately." — Acts 18:26_

#### Facts
- Paul stays in Corinth for some time, then sails for Syria with Priscilla and Aquila, stopping briefly at Ephesus where he leaves them.
- A Jewish believer named Apollos arrives in Ephesus; he is an eloquent man with a thorough knowledge of the Scriptures, teaching accurately about Jesus, though he knows only the baptism of John.
- Priscilla and Aquila hear him speak boldly in the synagogue, take him aside privately, and explain the way of God more accurately.
- Apollos then travels to Achaia (Corinth), where he vigorously refutes Jewish opponents in public debate, proving from the Scriptures that Jesus is the Messiah.

#### Insights
- Apollos demonstrates a beautiful combination of advanced eloquence and teachable humility. Despite being an expert communicator, he willingly accepts correction and deeper instruction from a tentmaking couple.
- Priscilla and Aquila model constructive discipleship. They do not publicly call out Apollos's incomplete theology; instead, they invite him home to invest in him privately.

#### Deeds
- Cultivate a teachable spirit. No matter how much you know or how long you have walked in faith, be open to learning and expanding your understanding through others.
