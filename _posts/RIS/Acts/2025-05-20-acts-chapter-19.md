# The Book of Acts
---

## Chapter 19
---

### 19.1 · The Holy Spirit at Ephesus and Miracles (19:1–22)

**Key verse:** _"In this way the word of the Lord spread widely and grew in power." — Acts 19:20_

#### Facts
- While Apollos is at Corinth, Paul travels to Ephesus and finds twelve disciples who had received only John’s baptism; Paul baptizes them in the name of Jesus, places his hands on them, and they receive the Holy Spirit.
- Paul speaks boldly in the Ephesian synagogue for three months, but due to stubborn opposition, he moves to the lecture hall of Tyrannus, teaching daily for two years so that all of Asia hears the word.
- God performs extraordinary miracles through Paul, including healing people via handkerchiefs and aprons that touched him.
- Seven sons of Sceva, a Jewish chief priest, try to drive out evil spirits using Jesus’s name superstitiously; the demoniac beats them severely, causing widespread fear and a public burning of valuable sorcery scrolls.

#### Insights
- Superficial religious formulas do not carry spiritual authority. The sons of Sceva tried to hijack the name of Jesus as a magical spell, but spiritual power requires a personal relationship, not just mimicking an vocabulary.
- Genuine revival results in a radical break from past sins. The believers in Ephesus didn't just stop practicing sorcery; they publicly burned their expensive scrolls, prioritizing God over monetary value.

#### Deeds
- Look for any "compromise areas" or cultural idols you have kept around out of convenience or value. Take decisive action to remove them completely from your life.

### 19.2 · The Riot in Ephesus (19:23–41)

**Key verse:** _"He says that gods made by human hands are no gods at all. And there is danger not only that our trade will lose its good name, but also that the temple of the great goddess Artemis will be discredited..." — Acts 19:26–27_

#### Facts
- A silversmith named Demetrius, who makes silver shrines of Artemis, realizes that Paul’s preaching is hurting his business and ruining the reputation of the goddess.
- He rouses his fellow craftsmen into a fury, sparking a city-wide riot where the crowd drags Paul’s companions, Gaius and Aristarchus, into the massive theater.
- The crowd shouts "Great is Artemis of the Ephesians!" for two solid hours in a state of confusion.
- The town clerk finally quiets the crowd, pointing out that the men are not sacrilegious, and dismisses the assembly to avoid being accused of rioting by Rome.

#### Insights
- The gospel can be financially disruptive. When the kingdom of God advances, it naturally threatens industries built on vice, exploitation, or idolatry.
- Idolatry often dresses up its financial self-interest as religious devotion. Demetrius’s main concern was his wallet, but he weaponized the crowd's religious pride to achieve his economic ends.

#### Deeds
- Reflect on your economic footprint. Ensure that your professional investments and consumer choices do not conflict with or compromise your kingdom values.
