# The Book of Acts
---

## Chapter 20
---

### 20.1 · Through Macedonia and Greece; Eutychus Raised (20:1–12)

**Key verse:** _"On the first day of the week we came together to break bread. Paul spoke to the people and, because he intended to leave the next day, kept on talking until midnight." — Acts 20:7_

#### Facts
- After the Ephesian riot subsides, Paul leaves for Macedonia and Greece, traveling through the regions to encourage the believers.
- He stays three months in Greece, but a plot against him by Jews forces him to change travel plans and return via Macedonia.
- In Troas, the believers gather on the first day of the week to break bread; Paul preaches an exceptionally long sermon that stretches until midnight.
- A young man named Eutychus, sitting in a third-story window, falls into a deep sleep, tumbles out to his death, but Paul rushes down, throws his arms around him, and restores him to life.

#### Insights
- The early church early on established the pattern of gathering on "the first day of the week" (Sunday, the day of resurrection) for community, teaching, and communion.
- Eutychus’s story shows a very human side of the early church—long sermons, physical fatigue, and unexpected accidents—juxtaposed with the restorative power of God.

#### Deeds
- Value your church family gatherings. When you gather for worship, seek to be present both physically and mentally, looking out for others who might be on the fringes or struggling.

### 20.2 · Paul's Farewell to the Ephesian Elders (20:13–38)

**Key verse:** _"However, I consider my life worth nothing to me; my only aim is to finish the race and complete the task the Lord Jesus has given me—the task of testifying to the good news of God’s grace." — Acts 20:24_

#### Facts
- Paul sails down the coast and stops near Miletus, sending for the elders of the Ephesian church to meet him there.
- He delivers a deeply emotional farewell address, reminding them of his tears, hard work, humility, and complete transparency in preaching the whole counsel of God.
- He warns them that "savage wolves" will come from both outside and within the flock to distort the truth.
- Paul kneels and prays with them; they weep openly and hug him, deeply grieved by his statement that they would never see his face again.

#### Insights
- Spiritual leadership requires deep emotional investment and transparent vulnerability. Paul did not lead through detached bureaucracy, but through authentic relationships, hard work, and tears.
- The biggest threat to a church community often comes from within its own ranks. Vigilance and deep biblical foundation are essential to protect the community from distorted teachings.

#### Deeds
- Pray for your local church leaders this week. Ask God to give them spiritual vigilance, endurance, protection from false teaching, and a heart to serve the flock with integrity.
