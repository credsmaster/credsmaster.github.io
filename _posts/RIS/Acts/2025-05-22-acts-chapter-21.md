# The Book of Acts
---

## Chapter 21
---

### 21.1 · Journey to Jerusalem and Warnings (21:1–16)

**Key verse:** _"I am ready not only to be bound, but also to die in Jerusalem for the name of the Lord Jesus." — Acts 21:13_

#### Facts
- Paul and his companions sail via Cos, Rhodes, Patara, and Phoenicia, landing eventually at Tyre, where disciples warned him through the Spirit not to go to Jerusalem.
- They proceed to Caesarea and stay at the house of Philip the evangelist, who has four unmarried daughters who prophesy.
- A prophet named Agabus arrives from Judea, takes Paul’s belt, binds his own hands and feet, and declares that the Holy Spirit says the owner of this belt will be bound by Jews in Jerusalem and handed to Gentiles.
- The believers weep and beg Paul not to go, but Paul declares he is ready to die for Jesus, and they resign themselves to God's will.

#### Insights
- Discerning prophecy requires wisdom. The Holy Spirit accurately revealed the *consequences* of Paul going to Jerusalem (bonds and affliction), but the believers misinterpreted that data as a prohibition to stop him, whereas Paul saw it as a call to embrace suffering.
- Conviction overrides comfort. Paul's resolve shows that obedience to God often means walking straight into hardship rather than trying to avoid it.

#### Deeds
- Are you willing to follow God's direction even when you know it will cost you comfort, reputation, or safety? Examine your heart's resilience.

### 21.2 · Arrival in Jerusalem and Paul's Arrest (21:17–40)

**Key verse:** _"When the seven days were nearly over, some Jews from the province of Asia saw Paul at the temple. They stirred up the whole crowd and seized him..." — Acts 21:27_

#### Facts
- Paul arrives in Jerusalem and meets with James and the elders, reporting on his ministry among the Gentiles.
- To counter rumors that he teaches Jews to abandon the law of Moses, the elders advise Paul to join four men in a Jewish purification vow and pay their expenses.
- Paul agrees; however, toward the end of the seven days, Jews from Asia spot him in the temple, falsely accuse him of bringing a Greek (Trophimus) into the inner courts, and incite a riot.
- A mob drags Paul out of the temple and tries to kill him, but the Roman commander (tribune) and soldiers rush in, arrest Paul, bind him with chains, and lead him to the barracks while Paul asks to speak to the crowd.

#### Insights
- Paul is willing to compromise on cultural traditions (joining the vow) to build a bridge of peace, but he remains completely uncompromised on the core elements of the gospel.
- Religious legalism easily degenerates into murderous rage based on false assumptions. The crowd did not check facts; they operated out of raw prejudice and misinformation.

#### Deeds
- Guard yourself against making assumptions or reacting out of anger based on rumors. Commit to factual integrity and a peaceful heart in tense situations.
