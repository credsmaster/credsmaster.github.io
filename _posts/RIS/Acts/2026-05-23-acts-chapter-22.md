# The Book of Acts
---

## Chapter 22
---

### 22.1 · Paul Speaks to the Jewish Crowd (22:1–21)

**Key verse:** _"Then the Lord said to me, 'Go; I will send you far away to the Gentiles.'" — Acts 22:21_

#### Facts
- Standing on the steps of the Roman barracks, Paul addresses the angry Jewish crowd in Aramaic, instantly quieting them.
- He establishes his credentials: born in Tarsus, raised in Jerusalem, thoroughly trained under Gamaliel, and a zealous persecutor of the Way.
- He retells the story of his Damascus road encounter with Jesus, his blindness, and his healing and baptism through Ananias.
- He recounts a subsequent vision in the Jerusalem temple where the Lord explicitly ordered him to leave the city because his testimony would be rejected, sending him instead to the Gentiles.

#### Insights
- Your personal testimony is one of the most effective tools for sharing your faith. Paul didn’t start with abstract theology; he told his personal story of transformation from a religious legalist to a follower of Christ.
- Highlighting personal identity can build cultural connections. Speaking in their language and detailing his religious pedigree earned Paul a respectful hearing before introducing a challenging truth.

#### Deeds
- Take time to write down your own testimony in three simple parts: your life before Christ, how you encountered Him, and how your life changed afterward. Be ready to share it.

### 22.2 · Paul's Roman Citizenship Evoked (22:22–30)

**Key verse:** _"As they stretched him out to flog him, Paul said to the centurion standing there, 'Is it legal for you to flog a Roman citizen who hasn't even been found guilty?'" — Acts 22:25_

#### Facts
- The moment Paul mentions the word "Gentiles," the crowd erupts in anger, shouting that he should be wiped off the face of the earth and throwing dust into the air.
- The Roman commander orders Paul brought inside the barracks to be flogged and interrogated to find out why the crowd is so furious.
- As they tie him up for flogging, Paul asks the centurion if it is legal to flog an uncondemned Roman citizen.
- The centurion alerts the commander, who is terrified to realize he has put a natural-born Roman citizen in chains; the next day, he releases Paul and orders the Sanhedrin to assemble.

#### Insights
- Legalism cannot tolerate grace. The crowd was fine with Paul's story of a miracle, but the inclusion of the Gentiles drove them into a blind rage.
- It is completely appropriate to use your legal rights and citizenship for the protection of your life and the advancement of the mission. Paul did not seek martyrdom uselessly; he intelligently deployed his Roman citizenship.

#### Deeds
- Understand your rights and resources in your local society. Use them wisely and strategically to protect the vulnerable and advance the work of God's kingdom.
