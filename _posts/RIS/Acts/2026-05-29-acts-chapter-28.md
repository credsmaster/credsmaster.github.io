# The Book of Acts
---

## Chapter 28
---

### 28.1 · Ministry on Malta and Arrival in Rome (28:1–16)

**Key verse:** _"The islanders showed us unusual kindness. They built a fire and welcomed us all because it was raining and cold." — Acts 28:2_

#### Facts
- Once ashore, they discover the island is called Malta, and the local inhabitants show them unusual hospitality by building a warm fire.
- As Paul gathers a bundle of sticks and puts them on the fire, a viper driven out by the heat fastens itself onto his hand.
- The locals assume he is a murderer pursued by justice, but when Paul shakes the snake off into the fire with no ill effects, they change their minds and think he is a god.
- Paul heals the father of Publius (the chief official of the island) of dysentery, leading to the healing of all the sick on the island; three months later, they sail to Italy and Paul arrives in Rome, where he is allowed to live by himself with a soldier guarding him.

#### Insights
- Superstitious mindsets swing wildly between extremes. Just like the crowd at Lystra, the citizens of Malta go from viewing Paul as a cursed murderer to an immortal god within minutes. True stability rests in God's definition of us, not public opinion.
- Paul remains an active servant in every environment. Whether surviving a shipwreck, gathering simple sticks for a fire, or dealing with an unexpected snake bite, his hands are always busy doing good.

#### Deeds
- Avoid defining your worth by the fluctuating opinions of others. Focus your energy on serving practically wherever you land, even in unexpected or temporary settings.

### 28.2 · Paul Preaches Undeterred in Rome (28:17–31)

**Key verse:** _"He proclaimed the kingdom of God and taught about the Lord Jesus Christ with all boldness and without hindrance." — Acts 28:31_

#### Facts
- Three days after arriving, Paul calls together the local Jewish leaders in Rome to explain that he has done nothing against his people and is bound in chains because of the hope of Israel.
- They arrange a day to meet, and from morning till evening Paul explains, testifies to the kingdom of God, and tries to convince them about Jesus from the Law and Prophets.
- Some are convinced, but others refuse to believe, prompting Paul to quote Isaiah 6 about their calloused hearts and closed ears, declaring that God’s salvation has been sent to the Gentiles.
- For two full years, Paul stays in his own rented house, welcoming all who come to visit him, boldly preaching the kingdom of God and teaching about Jesus Christ without hindrance.

#### Insights
- The Book of Acts ends with an open, triumphant note: "without hindrance" (*akōlytōs* in Greek). Though the apostle is physically bound by chains and guarded by Rome, the Word of God remains entirely free and unstoppable.
- God's plan is always fulfilled, even if the itinerary looks completely unexpected. Paul finally reached his destination of Rome, not as an influential visiting tourist, but as a political prisoner in chains—yet his impact was massive.

#### Deeds
- Reflect on your current limitations (physical, financial, situational). Realize that your external chains cannot restrain the power of the Holy Spirit working through you. Speak the gospel boldly right where you are.
