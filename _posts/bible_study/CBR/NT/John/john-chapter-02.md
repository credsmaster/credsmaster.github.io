# Part 1 — The Book of Signs (Chapters 1–12)

## Chapter 2

---

### 2.1 · Wedding at Cana — First Sign (2:1–12)

**Key verse:** _Write your key verse here_

#### Facts
- The wedding at Cana is "the first of his signs" — John is deliberate that these are signs, not just miracles, pointing beyond themselves.
- Jesus initially seems to decline his mother's request ("My hour has not yet come"), yet he acts.
- Six stone water jars used for Jewish purification rites are filled — each holding 20–30 gallons — then transformed into wine.
- 

#### Insights
- Mary's instruction to the servants — "Whatever he says to you, do it" — is perhaps the most universally applicable command in the gospel.
- The sheer abundance (120–180 gallons of fine wine) speaks to the extravagant generosity of God's kingdom breaking in.
- The water jars are for purification. Jesus does not abolish the old order — he transforms it, filling it with something far richer.
- 

#### Deeds
- Where in your life are you bringing your need to Jesus but then not following through with what he says?
- Reflect on the abundance of this sign. Do you approach God expecting just enough, or expecting extravagance?

### 2.2 · Cleansing of the Temple (2:13–25)

**Key verse:** _Write your key verse here_

#### Facts
- John places the temple cleansing at the beginning of Jesus' ministry (the Synoptics place it at the end — both are theologically intentional).
- Jesus drives out the money changers and those selling sacrificial animals with a whip of cords.
- When asked for a sign of his authority, Jesus speaks of his body as the true temple.
- 

#### Insights
- The temple was meant to be a house of prayer for all nations — commerce had turned the court of the Gentiles into a marketplace, blocking access to God for outsiders.
- Jesus' zeal for his Father's house is instructive: worship matters. How it is conducted matters. God is not indifferent to the atmosphere in which he is sought.
- The disciples only understood the "temple of his body" saying after the resurrection (v.22). Much of what Jesus says is understood only in retrospect.
- 
- 

#### Deeds
- Ask: Are there things in my own "house of prayer" — my inner life, my church — that are blocking access to God for others?
- Notice that Jesus does not trust himself to the crowds who believe in him based on signs (v.24). Seek a faith rooted in the person of Christ, not just in what he does for you.
