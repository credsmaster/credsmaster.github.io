# Part 1 — The Book of Signs (Chapters 1–12)

---

## Chapter 4

---

### 4.1 · Woman at the Well — Living Water (4:1–26)

**Key verse:** _"Everyone who drinks of this water will be thirsty again, but whoever drinks of the water that I will give him will never be thirsty again." — John 4:13–14_

#### Facts
- Jesus travels through Samaria — a route most Jews avoided — and stops at Jacob's well near Sychar.
- The woman is a Samaritan (Jews and Samaritans had deep mutual hostility) and comes to the well alone at noon, suggesting social marginalisation.
- Jesus asks her for water, then offers her "living water." The conversation moves through misunderstanding to personal revelation.
- Jesus reveals her five husbands and current situation — she recognises him as a prophet, then considers whether he might be the Messiah.
- 

#### Insights
- Jesus crosses three barriers to speak with this woman: ethnic (Jew-Samaritan), gender (Jewish men did not address women publicly), and moral (her reputation). The gospel has no barriers.
- "Living water" is a double meaning — flowing spring water versus still water, but also the spiritual life Jesus gives. She starts with the physical and is drawn into the spiritual.
- Jesus' self-disclosure here — "I who speak to you am he" — is one of the clearest Messianic self-declarations in any gospel, and he makes it to an outcast Samaritan woman, not to Jerusalem's elite.
- 


#### Deeds
- Reflect on your own thirsts — the things you keep returning to that never finally satisfy. Bring them to Jesus explicitly.
- Ask: Is there anyone I am reluctant to share the gospel with because of social, cultural, or moral barriers?
-

### 4.2 · Worship in Spirit and Truth (4:19–26)

**Key verse:** _God is spirit, and those who worship him must worship in spirit and truth." — John 4:24_

#### Facts
- The woman raises the Samaritan-Jewish dispute about the correct place of worship: Mount Gerizim or Jerusalem.
- Jesus says the hour is coming — and now is — when true worship will not be tied to any physical location.
- True worshippers worship the Father "in spirit and truth."
- 


#### Insights
- Jesus does not dismiss the Jerusalem tradition as wrong, but declares it provisional. The whole temple-geography system was always pointing toward something beyond itself.
- "In spirit" — worship that is inward, Spirit-enabled, not merely ritual. "In truth" — worship that is grounded in who God actually is, and in the truth revealed in Christ.
- The fact that this teaching comes to a Samaritan woman, not the Jerusalem religious establishment, is itself a statement about who the new worshipping community will include.
- 
- 

#### Deeds
- Examine your own worship: Is it Spirit-dependent and truth-rooted, or has it become habitual and external?
- Pray that God would renew your capacity to worship him "in spirit and truth" this week.
-

### 4.3 · The Disciples Return & the Harvest Field (4:27–38)

**Key verse:** _Lift up your eyes, and see that the fields are white for harvest." — John 4:35_

#### Facts
- The disciples return with food and are surprised Jesus is speaking with a woman, though none asks why.
-Jesus says his food is to do the will of the one who sent him and to finish his work.
- He uses the harvest image: others have laboured, and the disciples are entering into their labour.
- 


#### Insights
- Jesus reframes nourishment — what sustains him is not bread but obedience and mission. This is a challenge to what we think of as sustaining.
- "Lift up your eyes" is a call to see people as Jesus sees them — ready, open, hungry — not as obstacles or problems.
- The harvest field in view is the Samaritan village the woman has just gone to alert. Jesus is pointing at the people walking toward them.
- 


#### Deeds
- Ask God to let you see the people around you through the lens of harvest, not inconvenience.
-Reflect on the "others have laboured" principle — you are building on prayer, witness, and faithfulness that came before you.

### 4.4 · Samaritans Believe (4:39–42)

**Key verse:** _We have heard for ourselves, and we know that this is indeed the Saviour of the world." — John 4:42_

#### Facts
- Many Samaritans believe because of the woman's testimony: "He told me all that I ever did."
- They urge Jesus to stay, and he remains two days.
- After hearing Jesus directly, they tell the woman: now they believe not because of her words alone, but because they have heard for themselves.
- 

#### Insights
- The woman's testimony was imperfect and partial — "could this be the Christ?" — yet it was enough to bring a whole village to investigate.
- The Samaritans' final confession — "Saviour of the world" — is a striking title, broader than any Jewish Messianic expectation. They grasp something the Jerusalem leaders will resist throughout the gospel.
- Personal encounter with Jesus always moves people beyond second-hand faith ("because of your words") to first-hand faith ("we have heard for ourselves").
- 

#### Deeds
- Your testimony does not have to be polished or complete. Be faithful with what you know, and trust Jesus to do the rest.
- Examine whether your own faith is still primarily inherited or second-hand, or whether it is genuinely your own.
-

### 4.5 · Healing of the Official's Son — Second Sign (4:43–54)

**Key verse:** _The man believed the word that Jesus spoke to him and went on his way." — John 4:50_

#### Facts
- A royal official from Capernaum comes to Jesus in Cana and begs him to heal his dying son.
- Jesus says, "Unless you see signs and wonders you will not believe" — a gentle rebuke of sign-dependent faith.
-Jesus simply says, "Go; your son will live." The official believes and leaves. His servants confirm the healing happened at the exact hour Jesus spoke.
- 

#### Insights
- This is a healing at a distance — no touch, no travel, just a word. The sign demonstrates the absolute authority of Jesus' word.
- The official's faith develops in stages: he believes enough to come, he believes the word Jesus speaks, and then the confirmation deepens the faith of his whole household.
- "Your son will live" — spoken without drama or ritual. Jesus' authority does not require performance.
- 

#### Deeds
- Reflect on whether your faith requires constant signs and confirmation, or whether you can trust the word of Christ even before you see the outcome.
- What situation in your life needs you to "go on your way" trusting his word, rather than waiting for visible proof first?
