# The Book of Acts
---

## Chapter 2
---

### 2.1 · The Holy Spirit at Pentecost (2:1–13)

**Key verse:** _"All of them were filled with the Holy Spirit and began to speak in other tongues as the Spirit enabled them." — Acts 2:4_

#### Facts
- On the day of Pentecost, the 120 are gathered when a sound like a rushing wind fills the house and tongues of fire rest on each one.
- They begin speaking in other languages, and Jews from across the diaspora hear them declaring the wonders of God in their own native tongues.
- The crowd is amazed; some mock and accuse them of drunkenness.

#### Insights
- Pentecost reverses Babel — where language was scattered to divide humanity (Gen 11), here it is multiplied to unite people in hearing God's praise. The Spirit is not a private spiritual experience; it is a public, outward, mission-oriented power.
- Fire and wind are classic Old Testament theophanies (Sinai, the burning bush). The Spirit's arrival is marked by covenant-weight.
- The fact that devout Jews from every nation were in Jerusalem is no accident — God chose Pentecost, a pilgrimage feast, to launch a global mission through gathered witnesses.

#### Deeds
- Ask: Is my experience of the Holy Spirit primarily inward and personal, or does it have an outward, others-directed character?
- Reflect on what "your own language" means spiritually — how does God's message reach people in ways they can truly understand?

### 2.2 · Peter's Sermon (2:14–41)

**Key verse:** _"God has made this Jesus, whom you crucified, both Lord and Messiah." — Acts 2:36_

#### Facts
- Peter stands and preaches the first Christian sermon, quoting Joel 2 to explain the Spirit's outpouring.
- He declares Jesus's death was according to God's set purpose, that God raised him from the dead, and that he has now been exalted to pour out the Spirit.
- About three thousand people respond, repent, and are baptised.

#### Insights
- Peter's sermon is structured around a fulfilment argument: what you are seeing was promised by the prophets, accomplished by Jesus, and confirmed by resurrection. The gospel is not a new religion — it is the completion of Israel's story.
- The crowd's question in v.37 — "Brothers, what shall we do?" — is the right response to genuine conviction. The gospel is not merely informational; it demands a response.
- Three thousand souls in one day is a dramatic reversal of the three thousand who fell in the golden calf incident (Ex. 32:28). The Spirit gives life where the law brought death.

#### Deeds
- How confident are you in presenting the gospel as the fulfilment of God's redemptive story? Study Peter's use of the Old Testament this week.
- When last did you hear a sermon that left you asking, "What shall I do?" Examine whether your preaching diet produces conviction, not just information.

### 2.3 · The Fellowship of the Believers (2:42–47)

**Key verse:** _"They devoted themselves to the apostles' teaching and to fellowship, to the breaking of bread and to prayer." — Acts 2:42_

#### Facts
- The new believers devote themselves to four things: apostolic teaching, fellowship, the breaking of bread, and prayer.
- Signs and wonders are done through the apostles; believers share possessions so no one is in need.
- They meet daily in the temple courts and in homes; the Lord adds to their number daily.

#### Insights
- The four marks of early church life (teaching, fellowship, breaking of bread, prayer) are not a programme — they are a portrait. They describe people intoxicated with a new life, not people keeping up with a church checklist.
- Generosity here is not commanded by a rule but flows from transformed hearts. It is a theological statement: what you believe about Christ changes what you do with your money.
- "The Lord added to their number daily" — growth is God's work. The church's role is faithfulness; the fruit is his.

#### Deeds
- Which of the four marks of Acts 2:42 is most underdeveloped in your life or community?
- Consider one concrete step toward greater generosity or hospitality this week — not out of duty, but as an overflow of what you have received.
