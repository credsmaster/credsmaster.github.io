# The Book of Acts
---

## Chapter 5
---

### 5.1 · Ananias and Sapphira (5:1–11)

**Key verse:** _"You have not lied just to human beings but to God." — Acts 5:4_

#### Facts
- Ananias and Sapphira sell property and secretly keep part of the proceeds while claiming to give the full amount to the apostles.
- Peter, by revelation, confronts Ananias — who falls dead immediately. His wife Sapphira, questioned separately three hours later, repeats the lie and dies likewise.
- Great fear falls on the whole church and all who hear about it.

#### Insights
- This passage sits immediately after Barnabas's generous act — the contrast is deliberate. The sin is not keeping some of the money (that was permitted, v.4) but staging a performance of greater sacrifice than was true. It is the sin of managing reputation before God.
- The severity of the judgment signals that the early church is a holy space — God is present in it with a seriousness that demands integrity. This is not the God of polite religion.
- "Great fear came upon all" — this is not terror that drives people away, but the fear of the Lord that purifies and aligns. Fear here is a grace.

#### Deeds
- Examine your own spiritual performance: is there a gap between the impression you give and the reality of your devotion? Bring that gap to God honestly.
- How does your community handle integrity failures? Do you treat the church as a holy community accountable to God, or merely a social organisation?

### 5.2 · The Apostles Heal Many (5:12–16)

**Key verse:** _"The apostles performed many signs and wonders among the people." — Acts 5:12_

#### Facts
- The apostles meet regularly in Solomon's Colonnade; no one outside dares join them casually, yet the people hold them in high honour.
- Large numbers believe and are added — both men and women.
- People bring the sick into the streets so that Peter's shadow might fall on them; even the sick from surrounding towns come and are healed.

#### Insights
- The combination of holy fear and magnetic witness is striking: the church is at once set apart and irresistibly attractive. Holiness does not repel genuine seekers — it draws them.
- Peter's shadow healing is not magic — it is an overflow of the Spirit's presence in a person. It points to what becomes possible when a human being is truly inhabited by God.
- The growth here — "more and more" — follows the judgment of Ananias and Sapphira, not a revival meeting. Internal holiness fuels external fruitfulness.

#### Deeds
- Does the health of your inner life with God overflow into visible effect on those around you? Ask God to make your life a conduit, not a container.

### 5.3 · The Apostles Persecuted (5:17–42)

**Key verse:** _"We must obey God rather than human beings!" — Acts 5:29_

#### Facts
- The high priest and Sadducees arrest the apostles and put them in jail; an angel releases them overnight and sends them back to the temple courts to teach.
- Re-arrested and brought before the Sanhedrin, Peter and the apostles declare they must obey God rather than men.
- Gamaliel, a respected Pharisee, counsels caution — several other movements died out when their leaders were killed; if this is of God, it cannot be stopped.
- The apostles are flogged, ordered not to speak in Jesus's name, and released. They leave rejoicing that they were considered worthy to suffer.

#### Insights
- "Obeying God rather than men" is not a general principle of civil disobedience — it is the specific response of those commanded not to speak of Jesus. The conflict is about allegiance to Christ, not political ideology.
- Gamaliel's pragmatic wisdom is not the same as faith — but it is used by God to protect the early church. God can deploy even cautious unbelief in the service of his purposes.
- The apostles rejoice in suffering (v.41). This is not masochism — it is a sign that their identity and worth are no longer tied to comfort, approval, or safety. Joy and hardship co-exist in the Spirit.

#### Deeds
- Is there any area of your life where social or professional pressure is quietly silencing your witness?
- Sit with verse 41: what would it take for you to leave a difficult situation "rejoicing"? What would that require of your theology of suffering?
